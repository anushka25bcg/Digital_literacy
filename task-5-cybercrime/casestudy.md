### Case Study: Identity Theft

Identity theft is a cybercrime in which an individual’s personal information is stolen and used without their consent, often for financial gain. This information may include names, phone numbers, bank details, or login credentials.

A typical identity theft incident occurs in several steps. First, the attacker collects personal data through methods such as phishing emails, fake websites, or data breaches. For example, a victim may receive an email that appears to be from a trusted service, asking them to log in. When the victim enters their details, the attacker gains access to their account. Next, the attacker uses this information to access bank accounts, make unauthorized transactions, or create fake profiles. In some cases, the stolen identity is used for scams or illegal activities.

Students and inexperienced internet users are common targets, as they may not always recognise suspicious links or messages. People who frequently share personal information online are also at higher risk.

The consequences of identity theft can be serious. Victims may suffer financial loss, damage to their reputation, and emotional stress. Recovering from such incidents can take time and effort. Preventive measures such as using strong passwords, enabling two-factor authentication, and being cautious online can significantly reduce the risk.
